01 · Foundations

What is DMR?

DMR — Digital Mobile Radio — is an open digital radio standard adopted by RAYNET for its efficiency, clarity and capability to carry two simultaneous conversations on a single 12.5 kHz channel.

📡
The Standard
DMR is an ETSI open standard (ETSI TS 102 361). First published in 2005, it is now used globally by emergency services, utilities and amateur radio operators.
🔀
Two Timeslots
DMR uses TDMA (Time Division Multiple Access) to split one 12.5 kHz channel into two independent timeslots — effectively doubling spectrum efficiency.
🔊
AMBE+2 Vocoder
Voice is encoded using the AMBE+2 codec at 3,600 bps. The result is clear, consistent audio even at weak signal levels where FM would be unreadable.
🆚
DMR vs FM
FM degrades gradually as signal weakens — DMR maintains full quality right to the coverage edge, then drops sharply. This is the "digital cliff" effect.
ℹ
Why RAYNET uses DMR
DMR repeaters can carry talkgroup-routed traffic nationally via the Brandmeister and RAYNET-UK networks, allowing Liverpool RAYNET to communicate with other groups during large-scale incidents without the need for additional infrastructure.

Timeslot discipline is critical
Transmitting on the wrong timeslot can disrupt national traffic or cause your transmission to be lost entirely. Always verify your codeplug channel is set to the correct TS before operating.
03 · Codeplug

The DMR Codeplug

A codeplug is the configuration file programmed into your DMR radio. It defines every channel, talkgroup, zone and scan list. Never operate a DMR radio with an unchecked codeplug.

📂
What it contains
Channels, talkgroups, RX groups, zones, scan lists, boot screen, and radio ID. Each radio must have a unique registered DMR ID from RadioID.net.
🔢
Your DMR ID
Each licensed operator registers for a unique 7-digit DMR ID at RadioID.net. Your ID is embedded in every transmission and identifies you globally.
🛡️
Colour Codes
DMR repeaters use a Colour Code (CC 0–15) similar to CTCSS in FM. Your channel must be set to the matching CC or the repeater will not respond.
