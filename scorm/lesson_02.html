The NATO Phonetic Alphabet

To ensure clarity over radio, RAYNET operators use the NATO phonetic alphabet. Every letter has a standard spoken word to avoid confusion between similar-sounding letters (e.g. B and D, M and N).

Standard Radio Procedure Words (Prowords)

Prowords (procedure words) are standardised terms used to control radio communications efficiently. You must use these — not informal alternatives.

ProwordMeaning
OVERI have finished transmitting and expect a reply
OUTI have finished — no reply expected. End of contact.
WAITI must pause — do not reply yet
WAIT OUTI must pause — I will call you back
ROGERI have received and understood your last message
WILCOI understand and will comply
SAY AGAINPlease repeat your last message (NOT "repeat")
CORRECTWhat you said is correct
NEGATIVENo / that is incorrect
SENDProceed with your message
STANDBYWait — I will attend to you shortly
⚠ Never say "REPEAT" over the radio — this is an artillery command meaning "fire again". Always say "SAY AGAIN".

Making a Radio Call

A standard radio call follows the format: [Called station] — [Calling station] — OVER

📻 Example:

"Liverpool Control, this is Checkpoint 3, G4ABC, OVER"

"Checkpoint 3, Liverpool Control, send your message, OVER"

"Liverpool Control, Checkpoint 3 — runner 247 passed checkpoint at 10:35 — OVER"

"Checkpoint 3, ROGER — runner 247 logged at 10:35 — OUT"

Radio Discipline

  • Always listen before transmitting — the channel may be in use
  • Keep transmissions brief and accurate
  • Speak clearly at a moderate pace — about 100 words per minute
  • Hold the PTT (push-to-talk) for one second before speaking
  • Never transmit music, profanity, or unidentified transmissions
  • Log all traffic — time, from, to, message summary
