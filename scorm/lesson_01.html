What is RAYNET?

RAYNET (Radio Amateurs' Emergency Network) is a volunteer organisation of licensed amateur radio operators who provide emergency communications support to the statutory emergency services and the community.

We work under the guidance of Ofcom's licensing framework and operate under a formal agreement with served agencies such as local authorities, police, ambulance, and mountain rescue teams.

ℹ RAYNET is affiliated to the Radio Society of Great Britain (RSGB) and has over 2,000 active members across the UK, organised into regional groups.

Liverpool RAYNET — Group 10/ME/179

Liverpool RAYNET covers Merseyside and supports a wide range of events and exercises, including:

  • Large public events (marathons, cycling events, air shows)
  • Multi-agency exercises with emergency services
  • Severe weather deployments
  • Community resilience activities

Our group trains regularly and maintains a pool of equipment and trained operators who can be deployed at short notice.

The RAYNET Operator's Role

As a RAYNET operator, you provide a communications service — not an emergency service. Your role is to relay accurate information between parties who cannot communicate by other means.

⚠ You are not first aid trained, a police officer, or a firefighter by virtue of being a RAYNET operator. Stay in your lane — communicate, do not intervene.

Key Responsibilities

  • Operate your radio station safely and legally under your Ofcom licence
  • Follow the Net Controller's instructions at all times
  • Use plain language — no codes, no jargon
  • Maintain an accurate log of all messages passed
  • Report to your Group Controller any operational issues

Ofcom Licence Requirements

All RAYNET operators must hold a valid Ofcom amateur radio licence. There are three levels:

LicenceMax PowerFrequency Access
Foundation10WLimited bands
Intermediate50WMore bands
Full400WFull access

You must only operate within the terms of your licence. Operating outside your licence conditions is a criminal offence under the Wireless Telegraphy Act 2006.

Message Handling & Precedence

RAYNET uses a standard message precedence system to prioritise traffic:

PrecedenceDescriptionExample
EMERGENCYImmediate threat to lifePerson in cardiac arrest, location needed
URGENTTime-critical but not life-threateningKey event staff relocation
WELFAREPersonnel welfare checksOperator reporting in safely
ROUTINEGeneral operational trafficResource requests, updates

Always state the precedence at the start of your message. Emergency traffic has absolute priority over all other net traffic.
